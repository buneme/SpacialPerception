Advantages and disadvantages of Autonomous Vehicles

Is it worth continuing to develop our autonomous vehicle systems?

By Hubert

Advantages

Previously, applications of autonomous technology were limited to the premium segment of the market as part of adaptive cruise control systems, however with the recent development of radar and lidar sensors, more autonomy is now available to more of the market. This allows for several applications to be included in areas such as:

  • Driver support systems that allow the car to manoeuvre safely in case of an emergency or otherwise. (e.g driver falling asleep)
  • Freight transportation
  • Increased accessibility to vehicles

Furthermore, with continued increase in autonomy of road vehicles there is a large opportunity to decrease fuel consumption by exploiting platooning where vehicles drive closely to each other, effectively decreasing wind resistance for following trucks. Another benefit of this is increased road safety, drivers would get less tired and be able to minimise effects of tiredness allowing them to work longer hours.

Disadvantages

Ethical and moral dilemmas are created, for example, how is a vehicle supposed to decide what to do in life-threatening situations? Are passengers meant to be protected over the risk to pedestrians? Automotive vehicles also have a possibility of changing the economy, can improve crop-yield to farmers however it may also put many truckers out of jobs too quickly; without giving them sufficient time to retrain or retire. Furthermore, in the case of an incident, it may be difficult to recover the reasoning behind the choices of an algorithm that depend on machine learning techniques due to their inherent complex structure.

Most modern day systems remain vulnerable to hacking, and autonomous navigation systems would not be any different, therefore there is real potential for serious damage to be caused at almost no cost to an operator. Drivers and operators of autonomous vehicles may feel their privacy is being stripped away with constant connectivity and tracking.

With a rise of autonomous vehicles, these networks may become efficient enough to become much more popular than healthier and less harmful travel alternatives such as walking or cycling.
